Amazon Music iOS App

I recently switched from Spotify to Amazon Music for its better sound quality. But I found some tiny issues in the micro-interactions of this newly developed app.

Problem

Playlist

When I open a playlist and tap on a song on it, the full description menu that contains a big album picture and a full set of buttons slides in from the bottom.

If I want to switch to another song on that list, I have to slide down to close the menu first, and then tap on another item of the playlist. Imagine if I want to switch quickly between multiple songs on the list, I will have to repeat this excessive operation again and again, which is not a pleasant experience.

Feedback & Visual Cues

I understand that the purpose of surfacing the full menu is to give the user feedback when a list item is tapped, telling them that song is being played. However, as a music streaming app, visual cues shouldn't be the primary medium of feedback, the SOUND should! Users could simply hear it instead of seeing it.

Plus, there have already been enough visual cues that can give the user feedback, for example, the animated audio bars and the thumbnail at the bottom of the screen.

Therefore, showing the full menu every time a list item is tapped is excessive, and it disrupts the smooth user flow of browsing through a playlist.

Solution

Well, this one is a simple fix. Just stop surfacing the full menu every time, and let it be triggered only when the thumbnail is tapped. In this way, the user can browse through songs on the list more efficiently, and jump over some of them quickly if needed.
