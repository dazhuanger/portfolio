Neighbourhood Sports

A mobile application that provides a simple way to find sports partners in local communities. It is intended to improve community engagement through recreational sports.

My roles:

User Interview
Data Analysis
Wireframing
Prototyping

Research and Analysis

Problem

The current platform of community centres fails to provide easy and simple ways to find sport partners locally. It features a cluttered and counterintuitive design, which is causing poor turnout to local parks and arenas.

User Assumptions

Young professional.

Plays sports after work or during the weekends.

Has trouble finding local players to play with at the neighbourhood parks or courts.

Prioritization

Big ideas were prioritized based on the impact and feasibility.

No brainer

High impact & feasibility


Our results showed that this would be feasible because community centres are already established and impactful because people will go to community centres.

Big bets

High impact


Gamification can boost player's motivation which will enhance the impact of this app.

Landing Page

A simple landing page for users to log in or create a new account to join the community.

Home Page

The home page features three may sections:

Upcoming Games are shown in large cards with detailed information, including game date & time, location, level and intensity.

Recent Searches section allows players to generate a search quickly using previous criteria.

Finally, the Recommended for you section provides players with sports event they might be interested to attend.

New Game Menu

A new game setting menu slides in when the purple button on the bottom nav bar is tapped.

Players can create a new game according to their schedule and set the level and intensity of the game to their likings. The level settings can guarantee that the event attracts players at the same skill level, and the intensity settings tell the players how intense the game will be.
