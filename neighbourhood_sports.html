Neighbourhood Sports

Overview

Neighbourhood Sports is a mobile application that provides a simple way to find sports partners in local communities. It is intended to improve community engagement through recreational sports.

Research and Analysis

Problem

The current platform of community centres fails to provide easy and simple ways to find sport partners locally. It features a cluttered and counterintuitive design, which is causing poor turnout to local parks and arenas.

User Assumptions

Since we built everything from scratch in this project, there wasn't much stuff that we can use as a reference. So we decided to make some assumptions before starting the research, just to set up a preliminary structure for this project. We can later validate our assumptions based on our research findings and adjust accordingly, if necessary.

-- Who could be the target users?

Young professionals. Why? Because students often have dedicated facilities at school and a group of classmates to play sports with.

-- What do they do?

Play sports after work or during the weekends. Why? Because they have a day job, the only time they can play would be after work or on weekends.

-- What problems do they have currently?

Have trouble finding local players to play with at the neighbourhood parks or courts. Why? Because we often see that the courts and fields in community centres are empty. So that could be the reason.

User Persona

Our research findings did validate some of our assumptions, and also uncovered some other user expectations that we had not thought about. A typical one was that they want to play with somebody who has a similar skill level. So we adjusted our project goals accordingly.

Evaluation

We then conducted another round of evaluation on the mid-fi wireframes with several users and gathered their feedback:

  • The flow and structure are too linear. It lacks of a way to navigate horizontally between different pages at the same level.
  • Some UI elements, for example, scroll bars and forms, are not ideal for mobile devices.

High-fi Prototype

This course project was finished in December 2018. The final deliverable was the mid-fi wireframes above. We did get some feedback on the mid-fi prototype from guest judges during our final presentation. However, we didn't have the opportunity to implement their suggestions.

Until recently, during this pandemic, I decided to work individually to revive this project by making a high-fidelity version for the iPhone X.

I made quite a lot of changes in the high-fi prototype based on the feedback we got. First and foremost, I improved the UI elements, making them more mobile-friendly. In addition, I compressed several separate pages into one single page, which makes the MVP more efficient and less redundant.

Landing Page

A simple landing page for users to log in or create a new account to join the community.

Home Page

The home page features mainly three sections:

Upcoming Games are shown in large cards with detailed information, including game date & time, location, level and intensity.

Recent Searches section allows players to generate a search quickly using previous criteria.

Finally, the Recommended for you section provides players with sports event they might be interested to attend.

New Game Menu

A new game setting menu slides in when the purple button on the bottom nav bar is tapped.

Players can create a new game according to their schedule and set the level and intensity of the game to their likings. The level settings can guarantee that the event attracts players at the same skill level, and the intensity settings tell players how intense the game will be.

Map View

Players can choose to type in the desired location in the search bar or to use the "Near me" button as a shortcut, to find locations around them quickly. As this app aims to help players find partners locally, the "Near me" button may come in handy.

An embedded map will show up when a location search is generated. It allows the player to see available locations and select one as the venue of the upcoming game.

Task Completed

After setting up the sport, location, date & time, level and intensity, the user can tap the "Create" button to create a new game. The newly created game will be shown on the homepage.
