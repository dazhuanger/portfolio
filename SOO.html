Special Olympics Ontario

This community partner project focused on improving the overall information architecture of the Special Olympics Ontario's main website by refining the organization, navigation and labelling system of the website.

My role: User Interview Usability Testing Wireframing

Research and Analysis

Problem

Special Olympics Ontario's services are poorly represented on the website due to inextensible information architecture that was used to build the site. The IA of the main website suffers from various problems:

  • Incoherent and outdated organization system
  • Inconsistent labeling system
  • Disconnected navigation system
  • Broken search functionality
  • Lack of unified design and style

Research Methods

Semi-structured interview with SOO staff members. It encourages two-way communication which will allow informants the freedom to express their views.


Findings:

  • Users do encounter multiple problems with the website
  • But they learned to cope with it
  • Sometimes they choose to call or email SOO staff

Usability test with five potential users. These participants have limited knowledge about SOO and their website. Such participant profile would help us to analyze the behaviour of less frequent users of the website.

Findings:

  • Heavy cognitive burden
  • Excessive depth of information architecture

Design

Card Sorting Study

To bridge the gap between user expectations and the current design of the website, we conducted a card sorting study with volunteers and staff members of Special Olympics Ontario at their volunteer conference.

This study sheds light on the design of the categories, global navigation and labelling system of the website.

Preliminary IA Schematic Diagram

With the insights drawn from the card sorting study, we rearranged the most important categories into the global navigation menu to make them more salient.

Tree Test

To verify our design and to finalize the preliminary IA schematic diagram, we conducted a tree test for our new global navigation menu. To ensure validity and reliability of the evaluation, we used the same tasks that we had used to identify pain points of users in the early phase of the project. They are:

  • You decide to volunteer for Special Olympics Ontario. How would you use the website to find out how to become a volunteer?
  • You decide to donate your old but fully functioning car to Special Olympics Ontario. How would you use their website to make a car donation?
  • You have a friend with an intellectual disability. You are looking for some activities that he can participate in for the coming weekend. Where can you find the information?
  • You are a swimming coach for a local chapter under Special Olympics Ontario. You are trying to find rules for swimming games on the website. Where would you look for the information?

The result of the tree test shows that our design has successfully increased the usability and findability of the website. The first 3 tasks were accomplished by all the 5 users while the 4th task saw a 80% success rate. The overall success rate is 95% as shown in the figure below. Therefore, the preliminary IA schematic diagram does not need to be modified. We can keep it as the final version.
