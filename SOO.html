Special Olympics Ontario Website Redesign

As a community partner project, this project focused on improving the overall information architecture of the Special Olympics Ontario's main website by refining the organization, navigation and labeling system of the website.

Research and Analysis

Problem Statement

Since its inception, SOO has grown tremendously, providing a wide range of new services. These services are, however, poorly represented on the website due to non-extensible information architecture that was used to build the site.

The IA of the main website suffers from various problems - lack of adaptability resulting in incoherent and outdated organization system, inconsistent labeling system, navigational disconnection between the main-site and new services’ pages and broken search functionality. Its text-heavy nature results in a cognitive overload which made the website inefficient and ineffective. There is also a severe lack of unified design and architectural style across the site’s various pages/links.

Primary Reasearch

  • Semi-structured Interviews

The three participants of the interview are staff members who have been working with the organization for at least 4 years. They constitute multiple segments of our user base, such as local community coordinators, parents and coach. They also vary regarding the number of years which they have used the website for. Their experiences and roles in the organization have established their representativeness of the overall targeted user population and suitability or the project.
