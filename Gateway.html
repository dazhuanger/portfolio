Gateway Modernization

Overview

Gateway is an online client information centre of one of the Big Five banks. It provides access to account information and online investing tools to the bank's high net worth clients. As a product designer, I led the modernization project of this platform, revamped its user flow and enhanced the overall user experience.

My role

Competitor Analysis, Journey Mapping, User Flow Design, Wireframing & Prototyping (Desktop & Mobile)

Outcome

Redesigned homepage with an interactive performance chart and a sortable portfolio detail table.

New eDocuments retrieval and preferences page that increased the subscription rate of electronic documents.

Interactive performance reporting page to replace the current paper-based performance reports.

Spyglass mode that facilitates the communication between clients and investment advisors.

Last but not least, responsive versions for all the features above in the Gateway native app.

Discovery

Who is the user?

Gateway is a platform for the bank's high-net-worth clients. Most Gateway users are around 55-65 years old, with 1 - 2 million dollars managed by the bank's investment advisors. They use Gateway at least twice a week and visit more frequently during tax season. They are market informed and have an intermediate to expert level of knowledge. They typically focus on long-term trends instead of daily movements.

What is the problem?

In a nutshell, Gateway is an outdated platform that needs an overhaul. Stakeholders have been asking for a change for a long time, and they all have different expectations of it. As a product designer, my role is to address these needs smartly and find the best way to balance them.

Analysis

Clients are asking for a responsive version of Gateway, which is not available on the current platform. Data from analytics shows that 80% of users are visiting either from a phone or a tablet. Also, they are expecting to see more translated information other than hard-to-digest raw data.

Investment advisors want to see what their clients are seeing. 100% of advisors we interviewed have mentioned the discrepancy between a client view and a staff view of Gateway has created troubles when communicating with clients.

As for the business goals, the bank's executives want to reduce costs and promote new services, while maintaining high client and employee satisfaction.

Design

Design system

The bank has a very comprehensive design system. Therefore, I didn't have to go through the paper sketches and mid-fi wireframes. Every design iteration was created directly in Sketch using the existing components.

Feature 1: eDocuments

eDocuments service is the most frequently used feature on Gateway. The major pain-points users have are:

  • Hard to find the documents they want to see

    • The information architecture needs a redesign. On the legacy system, the eDocuments settings are under the "My Account" tab, which is confusing for the users.

  • Cumbersome user flow for eDocuments preferences settings

    • The legacy system only supports account-level settings. The user flow of adjusting the preferences is cumbersome and tedious. If a user has multiple accounts, they will have to go through each of their accounts.

First Iteration

For the first iteration of the new eDocument preference page, I grouped the accounts by categories and offered a global level setting. Users will no longer need to go through each of their accounts to change the settings.

User testing feedback

I worked with our researcher to gather a round of feedback from other stakeholders, and also conducted an unmoderated user testing on usertesting.com

Second iteration
  • New "Go Paperless" button

    • As the global level card confuses the user, I replaced it with a button that has the same function as the card. Users will be able to subscribe to eDocuments and alerts with just one click. And the button is a one-time shot. Once clicked, it will be disabled so that the user cannot unsubscribe so easily (which fulfills the business requirement). If they want to customize the settings for an individual account or a category of account, they can uncheck the corresponding checkbox.

  • Prompt for the user

    • To address the issue of users forgetting to save the change, I added a prompt that shows up when there is an unsaved change. This is a trade-off after consulting our tech lead. Since the bank's back-end system cannot support frequent visits, the ideal way of dynamically saving changes when users check or uncheck a checkbox is not feasible.

  • Better Information Architecture

    • Lastly, the second iteration also improved the information architecture of the web app. Now users have a dedicated tab on the top nav bar to access their eDocuments.
